About Me

KC

I'm pursuing an MSE in Electrical Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania through the Vagelos Integrated Program in Energy Research. My work focuses on pushing the boundaries of what's possible in semiconductor devices and autonomous systems.

Currently, I'm researching ferroelectric diodes for next-generation non-volatile memory at the Jariwala Lab, where I'm co-first-authoring a paper on multilevel AlScN ferroelectric diodes in crossbar configurations. I also work on autonomous vehicle systems at the Mangharam Lab, developing ROS2-based control stacks.

Beyond research, I'm a TA for programming courses, work on aerospace projects with Penn Aerospace Club, and won $10k at the Citadel-CorrelationOne Datathon. I love projects that bridge hardware and software‚Äîfrom VLSI design to ML applications.
